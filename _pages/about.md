- This study examines heterogeneous carbon peaking pathways across 329 Chinese cities (1992–2023) by constructing a comprehensive emission dataset using a novel Data Alignment Technique. Combining machine learning and econometric models, we identify fragmented and asynchronous peaking dynamics, with nearly two-thirds of cities yet to reach their carbon peak. Distinct regional clusters and staged emission reduction mechanisms—shifting from energy restructuring to technological innovation and policy regulation—are observed among Peak Cities. The findings highlight the necessity of coordinated yet differentiated climate strategies to promote technological diffusion and policy synergy across city agglomerations.

[Does Green Finance Causally Reduce Carbon Intensity in Cities? Evidence from 285 Cities in China.]

Xueyang Li, **Jinlei Ma**<sup>†</sup>

- Combining econometric models and machine learning analysis, this study demonstrates that green finance significantly reduces city-level carbon intensity, with green bonds and investment showing the strongest and spatially spillover effects. The decarbonization impact operates mainly through energy restructuring and industrial upgrading and is more pronounced in technologically lagging and coal-dependent cities, underscoring the importance of differentiated green finance systems..
